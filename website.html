Introduction

Esports is the world of competitive, organised video gaming. Competitors from different teams (or individuals) face off playing popular games. These games are broadcasted and watched by millions of fans across the world. The fans are able to attend the tournament and watch or view at home. Some interesting facts about eSports are that it's expected to reach 600 millions occasional viewers and esports enthusiasts by 2020, the lowest to highest earning made from an eSport player is from $75,000 to $2 million. What Is Esports?

Esports is a form of competition in video games. It often takes place in organised competition, in the form of a ranked game mode, which everyone is able to access, or tournaments, where professional gamers compete. Popular video game genres in Esports are first person shooters, multiplayer online battle arena, fighting, cardgames, battle royales, and real-time strategy. By 2010, esports was important in the videogame industry and continues to be that role in the industry.

Who Started Esports?

In 1952 there was a man who studied at the University of Cambridge named Alexander Shafto Douglas who created the extremely popular game of “Tic-Tac-Toe”. This was the very first competitive pc game where a person would goes against the computer. He created this game because he was studying the interaction between a person and a computer and thought it would be a great idea to have a video game where they would play against each other. But Atari was the to start esports launching a tournament with 10 thousand people.

Why Did Esports Start?

Esports started because gamers who were very talented wanted to be rewarded for their skills. They played as intensely as an NHL player but would make way less money. Organizations started making esport teams for various games and the companies for those games would host tournaments with large prize pools. Gamers would also make money from their youtube channels and their streaming on twitch. With these things combined players could now have a pretty decent income off of playing video games for a living.

Where Did Esports Take Place?

Esports is popular in regions such as North america, Europe, and Asia. Esports first took off in Asia, mainly China and South Korea. Many of the first tournaments began in asia. Esports tournaments take place in regions, and sometimes worldwide. Esports broadcasts take place on twitch or youtube, and broadcasted on live television.

When Did Esports Start?

The first official esports event was in 1972. It was for the game Spacewar, where students from Stanford University competed in games to earn a prize. Then in 1980 a Space Invaders championship that held 10k participants was held showing the growth of gaming during the last 8 years.

How does Esports affect our society

With many technological advances, eSports has become a lifestyle for many people. Before, someone who played video games was often seen as lazy or useless. Times have changed and now many people are looking for a place in any professional league to compete. As of 2018, the esports industry was estimated to be worth 138 billion dollars. Esports continues to grow as new competitive games are made, and people begin to find a passion for gaming.

eSports History to What it is Now

Nov 1, 1980 was the beginning of esports and when the first video game tournament took place. Jan 1, 1990 was when pc gaming and online play started. The first Nintendo World Championships also happened that same year. On Jan 1, 2002 MLG was founded and eSports became a mainstream event. On Oct 13, 2012 League of legends had its Season 2 World Championship with a prize pool of around 2 million dollas. On Jul 13, 2013 League of Legends was accepted as an Olympic Sport.
